Research and Work

My research is centered around various environmental policy and justice dimensions. Some recent work is devoted to examining energy policy, particularly focusing on the implications of fracking and oil extraction on environmental justice within the U.S. My primary interests, however, are in collaborative governance, specifically in the realm of water policy.

One of my ongoing projects delves into analyzing policy outputs and outcomes derived from collaborative watershed management efforts. The primary objective of this study is to understand collaborative actions on water quality. This national-scale project examines collaborations across multiple watersheds throughout the U.S., constituting an extension of my dissertation research.
