I'm a faculty member in the Division of Politics, Administration & Justice at Cal State Fullerton. I study and teach about important things — public administration, public policy, and collaborative governance. I enjoy my work. I came to Cal State Fullerton after getting my Ph.D. in Public Policy and Public Administration from Auburn University.

I teach because I like it. The students are eager, full of ideas. We learn together. Public policy is complex, facinating. Public administration is a crucial discipline. Collaborative governance is an evolving field. My research lies at the intersection of these areas — where public policy crosses paths with networks of actors and community involvement. Water policy is my focus. I study the outcomes of working together, of shared governance. It's real. It's important. It shapes our world.

Apart from my professional life, family is important. My children and pets, they mean the world to me. We share time. Indoors, outdoors, it doesn't matter. What matters is being together. Volunteering matters, too. Helping organizations, student clubs, it's a good thing. Gives purpose, builds community.

I like Southern California. It's a melting pot, full of diversity. People, landscapes, foods — there's richness everywhere. It's different from home, but I've grown to love it. But there's something about home that never leaves you. The people, the authors, they're part of me. They tell their stories, stories of the South. The Bitter Southerner, that's where you'll find those stories. Stories of home.
