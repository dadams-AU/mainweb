About me About

I work in the Division of Politics, Administration & Justice at California State University, Fullerton. It's a good place. I study important things — public policy, public administration, collaborative governance.

I teach. I like teaching. The students are eager, full of ideas. We learn together. Public policy is complex, fascinating. Public administration is a crucial discipline. Collaborative governance is an evolving field.

My research, it's about the meeting points. Where public policy crosses paths with community involvement. Where it finds environmental justice. Water policy is my focus. I study the outcomes of working together, of shared governance. It's real. It's important. It shapes our world.

I came to Cal State Fullerton after Auburn University. That's where I earned my Ph.D. in Public Policy and Public Administration. My work is fulfilling. The journey, too.

Family is important. My children and pets, they mean the world to me. We share time. Indoors, outdoors, doesn't matter. What matters is being together.

I like Southern California. It's a melting pot, full of diversity. People, landscapes, foods – there's richness everywhere. It's different from home, but I've grown to love it.

Volunteering matters to me. Helping organizations, student clubs, it's a good thing. Gives purpose, builds community.

Home. There's something about home that never leaves you. The people, the authors, they're part of me. They tell their stories, stories of the South. The Bitter Southerner, that's where you'll find those stories. Stories of home.
