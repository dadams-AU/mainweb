About Me

I'm a faculty member in the Division of Politics, Administration & Justice at Cal State Fullerton. My work focuses on public administration, public policy, and collaborative governance—fields that I'm deeply passionate about. After earning my Ph.D. in Public Policy and Public Administration from Auburn University, I joined the faculty at Cal State Fullerton, where I continue to explore and teach these important subjects.

Lately, my interests are gravitating towards the integration of blockchain, IoT, and open-source technology in the field of water resource governance. I believe these cutting-edge technologies have the potential to revolutionize the way we approach governance by enhancing transparency, collaboration, efficiency, adaptability, and decentralization. Drawing on my understanding of adaptive management and polycentric governance, I'm excited to explore how these innovations can lead to more resilient, equitable, and sustainable water management practices. It's a thrilling time to be part of this rapidly changing world, and I'm eager to contribute to shaping the future..

More About Me

I teach because I love it. The students are enthusiastic, filled with fresh ideas, and together we delve into the complexities of public policy, administration, and collaborative governance. My research focuses on water policy and explores the intersection where public policy meets community involvement. The study of collaboration and shared governance is real, vital, and it shapes the world in which we live.

My family is where I put most of my love and energy. My children and pets are my world, and spending time with them is what truly matters. Volunteering also holds a special place in my heart. Whether it's helping organizations, student clubs, or friends, contributing to the community gives purpose to life. It grounds me and gives me strengh. And it's where good, honest learning emerges.

Living in Southern California, I've come to appreciate the rich diversity in people, landscapes, and cuisine. It's different in some ways from where I spent most of my life, and its uniqueness has captured my affection. My roots run deep in the South, though. It's slow times, slow food, and long stories. Southerners tell the best stories and cook the best food. There's a lot of richenss in the South. In the stories. In the people. Read about it, cook it, learn some culture on one of my favorite websites: The Bitter Southerner — it's is a treasure trove.
