About Me

I'm a faculty member in the Division of Politics, Administration & Justice at Cal State Fullerton. I study and teach important things — public administration, public policy, and collaborative governance. I enjoy my work. I came to Cal State Fullerton after getting my Ph.D. in Public Policy and Public Administration from Auburn University.

More About Me

I teach because I like it. The students are eager and full of ideas. We learn together. Public policy is complex, fascinating. Public administration is a crucial discipline. Collaborative governance is an evolving field. My research lies at the intersection of these areas — where public policy crosses paths with networks of actors and community involvement. Water policy is my focus. I study the outcomes of working together, of shared governance. It's real. It's important. And it shapes our world.

Family is more important than work. My children and pets, they mean the world to me. We share time. Indoors, outdoors, it doesn't matter. What matters is being together. Volunteering matters, too. Helping organizations, student clubs, friends. That's the good stuff. It gives purpose and builds community.

I like Southern California. It's a melting pot, full of diversity. People, landscapes, foods — there's richness everywhere. It's different from where I spent most of my life, and I've grown to love it. But there's something about home that never leaves you. The food, I cook it. The people, the authors, they're part of me. I like to read their stories. The Bitter Southerner, that's where you'll find some of those stories. Stories of the South.
