About Me

I'm a faculty member in the Division of Politics, Administration & Justice at Cal State Fullerton. My work focuses on public administration, public policy, and collaborative governanceâ€”fields that I'm deeply passionate about. After earning my Ph.D. in Public Policy and Public Administration from Auburn University, I joined the faculty at Cal State Fullerton, where I continue to explore and teach these important subjects.

Lately, my interests are gravitating towards the integration of blockchain, IoT, and open-source technology in the field of water resource governance. I believe these cutting-edge technologies have the potential to revolutionize the way we approach governance by enhancing transparency, collaboration, efficiency, adaptability, and decentralization. Drawing on my understanding of adaptive management and polycentric governance, I'm excited to explore how these innovations can lead to more resilient, equitable, and sustainable water management practices. It's a thrilling time to be part of this rapidly changing world, and I'm eager to contribute to shaping the future.

More About Me

I teach because I love it. The students are enthusiastic, filled with fresh ideas, and together we delve into the complexities of public policy, administration, and collaborative governance. My research focuses on water policy and explores the intersection where public policy meets community involvement. The study of collaboration and shared governance is real, vital, and it shapes the world we live in.

Despite my professional commitments, family comes first. My children and pets are my world, and spending time with them is what truly matters. Volunteering also holds a special place in my heart. Whether it's helping organizations, student clubs, or friends, contributing to the community gives purpose to life.

Living in Southern California, I've come to appreciate the rich diversity in people, landscapes, and cuisine. It's a remarkable contrast to where I spent most of my life, and its uniqueness has captured my affection. Yet, the connection to my roots remains strong. Whether it's cooking cornbread and catfish or reading stories from some of the world's best storytellers, a part of me always resonates with the South. For those interested in Southern stories, The Bitter Southerner is a treasure trove.
